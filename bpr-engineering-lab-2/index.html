About BPR Engineering Lab

BPR Engineering targets individuals seeking to gain traffic, safety, and transportation engineering industry - related experience. The bootcamp provides a structured learning pathway designed to bridge academic knowledge with real-world application. Participants are exposed to essential concepts, practical tools, and analytical techniques used by practicing transportation engineers.

The program focuses on developing core competencies in traffic operations analysis, roadway safety evaluation, transportation planning, and engineering documentation. Through hands-on exercises, simulation software exposure, case studies, and mentorship, attendees gain practical insight into how transportation solutions are developed, analyzed, validated, and implemented.

The bootcamp is ideal for:

  • Recent graduates seeking practical experience.
  • Career changers entering transportation engineering.
  • Junior engineers aiming to upskill.
  • Professionals preparing for PE licensure.
  • Anyone wanting hands-on industry exposure.

By the end of the program, participants will have:

  • A foundational understanding of traffic engineering principles.
  • Familiarity with analysis tools such as Synchro, SIDRA, VISSIM, and safety predictive methods.
  • Exposure to real project workflows, QC/QA expectations, and reporting standards.
  • Confidence to contribute meaningfully to engineering projects.

Ultimately, the bootcamp equips attendees with the practical knowledge, software awareness and analytical mindset needed to succeed and grow in consulting, government agencies or research settings.

We Will Covering

Predictive Safety Analysis (HSM / IHSDM)

Learn how to apply Highway Safety Manual methodologies, NCHRP and HSM tools (IHSDM) to estimate crash frequency and quantify safety benefits.

Crash Analysis & Crash Data Analytics

Work with real crash datasets to identify trends, diagnose contributing factors, and develop evidence-based safety insights and recommendations.

Traffic Volume Development

Gain practical experience in reviewing traffic data, developing and balancing traffic volumes, and applying growth projections for operational and safety analysis.

Freeway Capacity Analysis

Conduct freeway operations assessments from start to end, evaluate Level of Service, and interpret performance results.

Microsimulation with VISSIM

Learn how to build, code, and analyze microsimulation models, including calibration techniques and visualization methods for real-world problem solving.

Synchro Intersection Analysis

Set up Synchro models, optimize signal timing, and evaluate delays, queues, and intersection performance for existing and future conditions.

Introduction to SIDRA Intersection

Explore SIDRA software with emphasis on roundabout analysis.
